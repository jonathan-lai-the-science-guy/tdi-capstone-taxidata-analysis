About "which bars are worth a taxi ride?"

"Which bars are worth a taxi ride" is the brain-child from Jonathan Lai.

When it comes to picking restaurants, bars, and other forms of entertainment, Jonathan is completely indecisive. Because of his busy schedule and his low standards for selecting food establishments, he often asks his friends and family for suggestions for either lunch or dinner.

To remedy this problem, Jonathan has tried to use several food suggestion and review applications; however, he noticed that many of the applications used rating systems based on qualitative reviews instead of quantitative metrics. Because Jonathan wanted a quantifible heurstic to determine which food establishment were good, he decide to find a proxy for business traffic-real vehicular traffic. By embracing the decision making power found in millions of taxi rides, Jonathan knew he could get a rough estimate for a business success.
