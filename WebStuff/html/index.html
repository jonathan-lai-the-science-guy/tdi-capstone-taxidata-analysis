Where do young (and affluent) people in NYC live?

Let's say you own a bar in Manhattan and are trying to reach out and tap into the young professional market. These young professionals might not have the wealth nor capital to afford the Manhattan rent and instead are living in either Williamsburg or Long Island City. And after a long night of drinking, they are probably going to hail a cab home. With our backend tools, you can identify where these cab rides are going and focus your marketing campaign on this particular segment—maximizing both your market penetration and exposure.

Alternatively, you might be opening up a new bar in NYC and you need tools to find the perfect location. The biggest barrier to building a bar is getting a liquor license and the biggest barrier to getting a liquor license is getting approval from the community board. The easiest place to open a bar is probably in commerical zones (with friendly boards) with the largest return-on-investment (ROI). Our data has a fine enough resolution to know that it is better to build a bar on Briggs and Broadway (by the bridge) than anywhere else in Williamsburg. The overall increase in vehicular traffic will justify the increased rental pricing.
